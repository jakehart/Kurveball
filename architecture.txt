It's vitally important that smoothly syncing speeds between velocity curves is as easy as possible, and all gotchas are handled for the user as reasonably as they can be. Looking up a sync point is easy enough, but where there's a gap due to the geom of the curves, a sudden cutover can feel abrupt. therefore, syncing should always have a blend time. the user can say zero time for an instant switch, or have a blend to bridge that gap.

================================= ADVANCED IDEAS =================================
NETWORKING
see recording section below. p much just a matter of sending over the recorded curve and spline to be replayed. can generate splines from the rolling buffer of incoming positions - see "the continuity of splines" by freya
teams-esque time stretch/squeeze
expose easy-to-use nodes that can hook into unreal's networking

CURVE AND SPLINE RECORDING
continue to take UpdateVelocityCurve calls every frame, which is required for truly responsive input
BUT have optional functionality that records a small rolling buffer of the positions, finds a best-fit spline segment for them. then make a curve to traverse that spline with the same feel, taking the same time. prioritize replayability -- meaning that the generated spline and curve should play out in such a way that they take the exact same amount of time to complete as the original real motion, and end at the exact same destination. the speed can be fudged slightly to achieve this (dependent variable).
and the final recorded curve result might be obtained by multiplying discrete speedMultiplier changes against the original curve data
this is kinda like breadcrumbing, but more integrated with the curve/spline system and more explicit.
ALSO this might be a slippery slope but i could even make it possible to record motion data from other games using a memory location found with cheatengine
could even record motion from video of real vehicles e.g. cars on racetracks to perfectly replicate their handling

DETERMINISM
